## 2024-05-14 - Making SwiftUI List Rows Fully Clickable
**Learning:** Wrapping a SwiftUI `HStack` inside a `Button` only makes the child elements within the `HStack` clickable. Empty space within the `HStack` will ignore taps by default, resulting in a poor UX with small hit targets in list rows. Additionally, VoiceOver will treat each element separately.
**Action:** Always apply `.contentShape(Rectangle())` to the `HStack` when wrapping it in a `Button` to ensure the entire row area acts as a single large hit target. Apply `.buttonStyle(.plain)` to avoid default blue tinting on text. Use `.accessibilityElement(children: .combine)` to ensure screen readers announce the row as a single cohesive element, and append state traits like `.accessibilityAddTraits(.isSelected)` to convey proper semantics to VoiceOver.
