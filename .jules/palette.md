## 2024-05-14 - Wide Hit Targets for List Rows
**Learning:** Wrapping an entire list row's `HStack` in a `Button` with `.contentShape(Rectangle())` drastically improves the usability of the row compared to only having a small clickable element. This also greatly benefits screen reader users by allowing `.accessibilityElement(children: .combine)` and state-based traits to make the row read as a single, toggleable control.
**Action:** When creating clickable list rows in SwiftUI, ensure a wide hit target by wrapping the entire row contents in a `Button`, applying `.contentShape(Rectangle())` to make empty space clickable, using `.buttonStyle(.plain)` to prevent unintended text styling, and adding `.accessibilityElement(children: .combine)` along with state-appropriate traits like `.accessibilityAddTraits(.isSelected)`.
